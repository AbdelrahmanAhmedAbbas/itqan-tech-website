## Design Context

### Users
- **Primary audience:** Founders and startup leaders with validated SaaS ideas who need a technical partner to build production-ready products
- **Context:** They've likely been burned by agencies before. They're evaluating Itqan against other dev shops and freelancers. They're spending $15K-$80K and need confidence before committing
- **Job to be done:** Find a trustworthy, senior technical team that can turn their idea into a scalable product — fast, without the typical agency runaround
- **Bilingual audience:** English and Arabic speakers (full RTL support), serving clients across 3+ countries

### Brand Personality
- **3 words:** Precise, Elegant, Trusted
- **Voice:** Confident but never arrogant. Direct, no-fluff language. Speaks like a senior engineer who respects your time
- **Tone:** Calm authority — we've done this before, and we'll do it right
- **Name meaning:** "Itqan" (إتقان) means "mastery" or "perfection" in Arabic — the brand name IS the promise

### Emotional Goals
- **Primary:** "I can trust these people" — calm confidence, clarity, professionalism that removes doubt. Visitors should feel safe handing over their idea
- **Secondary:** "Finally, someone who gets it" — relief and connection. They've been burned before, and this feels different
- **NOT:** Hype, urgency, or flashy showmanship. Never "growth-hack bro" energy

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone:** Dark precision meets premium whitespace. The intersection of Linear/Vercel (sharp, developer-focused, subtle animations) and Apple/Stripe (clean, spacious, refined details)
- **Color strategy:** Dark hero (Void #09090B) transitions to light body (Snow #FAFAFA). Accent blue (#185ADB — Classic Tech Blue from logo) used sparingly for CTAs and highlights — never overwhelming
- **Typography:** Inter for clarity, Playfair Display italic for editorial moments, JetBrains Mono for technical credibility. Arabic counterparts: IBM Plex Sans Arabic + Noto Naskh Arabic
- **Shape language:** Large border-radius (2rem) for cards and nav, rounded-full for buttons and pills. Soft but not playful
- **Motion:** GSAP-powered, purposeful animations. Subtle ambient movement (orbs, floating nodes), staggered reveals on scroll. Never gratuitous — every animation should reinforce precision
- **Anti-references:** No gradients-everywhere Framer style. No playful/bouncy Lemon Squeezy energy. No cluttered agency templates. No stock photography

### Design Principles
1. **Precision over decoration** — Every element earns its place. If it doesn't clarify, guide, or build trust, remove it
2. **Quiet confidence** — Let the work speak. Restrained color, generous whitespace, and sharp typography convey mastery better than bold claims
3. **Clarity is respect** — The user's time matters. Clear hierarchy, scannable content, and obvious next steps. No cognitive load puzzles
4. **Craft in the details** — Micro-interactions, consistent spacing, thoughtful transitions. The kind of polish that signals "these people care about quality"
5. **Bilingual-native** — RTL is not an afterthought. Both languages should feel equally considered and equally beautiful

### Accessibility
- **Target:** WCAG AA compliance
- **Requirements:** Sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for text, 3:1 for large text/UI), keyboard navigation, screen reader support, semantic HTML, meaningful alt text
- **Motion:** Respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — provide static fallbacks for GSAP animations
- **Color:** Ensure information is never conveyed by color alone

### Technical Stack
- React 19 + Vite 8 + Tailwind CSS 3
- GSAP for animations
- lucide-react for icons
- i18n via custom LanguageContext (JSON translation files)
- No component library — custom components throughout
